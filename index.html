About Me

I'm passionate about the intersection of biology and AI. My background blends chemical engineering and systems biology, giving me a unique perspective on the challenges and opportunities in this space. I've conducted hands-on wet lab research, working with both in vitro and in vivo models, providing me with a deep understanding of biological systems.

To further support the research community, I developed an open-source software package for biological data analysis and share my expertise by lecturing annually at Stanford on Cloud Computing for Biology and Healthcare.

I'm particularly intrigued by the potential of large language models, especially multi-agent systems. I've successfully leveraged these to not only summarize complex biological data but also to review results and even generate novel hypotheses, significantly accelerating the research process.

My machine learning skillset is broad, covering ML classification for proteomics and genomics data, ML linear modeling for wearables data, and deep learning-based genomics models, such as predicting gene expression directly from DNA sequence.

Beyond technical skills, I'm a team leader with experience fostering collaboration and driving cross-functional research. I'm highly problem-solving oriented and consistently focus on delivering impactful, tangible results.
